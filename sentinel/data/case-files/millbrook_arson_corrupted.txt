---

INCIDENT REPORT

At 03:18 AM on 22 June 2019, a report of fire was received from a passing
motorist near 14 Industrial Way. Two units responded within nine minutes.
By arrival, the fire had engulfed the southeast corner of a disused warehouse.
No occupants were found inside the structure. The fire was contained at
05:40 AM after significant damage to the roof and eastern wall.

---

FORENSIC FINDINGS

Scene examination by the Fire Investigation Unit concluded the fire was of
deliberate origin. The point of origin was identified in the southeast corner
of the warehouse ground floor, consistent with a localised ignition event.

Ethanol-based solvent was confirmed as the primary accelerant, identified from
residue samples recovered at the origin point. Concentration levels indicated
deliberate application across an area of approximately four square metres.

Secondary residue traces consistent with ethanol-based solvent were recovered
from the northeast stairwell, suggesting the perpetrator entered and exited
via the building's rear access point.

---

INVESTIGATION NOTES

Three persons of interest were identified during the initial inquiry. None
could be placed at the scene by forensic or witness evidence. The warehouse
had been vacant for eight months prior to the incident and was pending
redevelopment. The owner reported no known disputes or prior threats.

CCTV coverage in the immediate area was limited. A partial plate number from
a vehicle observed near the site at 02:47 AM was not matched to any registered
vehicle in the county database.

---

CONCLUSION

Investigation suspended due to insufficient evidence. Case transferred to
cold case archive on 28 September 2019. Available for review upon request.
